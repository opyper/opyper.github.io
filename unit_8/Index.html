Katherine Mansfield's baby meal: baked eggs with cheese straws

I came across this meal in Katherine Mansfield's journals (from 1919) and can vouch for the fact that it is very soothing and best eaten only with the company of a cat. I sat with Alfie(my version of Athanaeum) hiding from the storm outside and passed a pleasant afternoon consulting various cookbooks on the correct consistency of cheese straws and wondering why no one will give me a job.

The end of the affair: falling in love over steak and onions

As Lent has just begun, and Valentines Day has just passed, I reached for the only book I know that deftly combines romance with Catholicism: Graham Greene's, The End of the Affair. In a moment laden with unspoken tension that begins their doomed affair, Maurice Bendrix and Sarah Miles tentatively acknowledge their feelings for one another over a plate of steak and onions in a Clapham restaurant. The rest, is history.

A moveable feast: shrimp, cassoulet & cafe ice-cream

Hemingway seemed to live his life from meal to meal, and his novels are awash with references to food. Obviously one can't overlook the fact that his characters also seem to be constantly imbibing some form of neat spirit, so for this meal alcohol abounded. It flowed, was spilt, drenched itself into clothing and carpets and cats. And we didn't care. We were hedonistic bohemians living from fiesta to fiesta, flinging Pernod down our throats with reckless abandon...
